Dr. Karla Valdivieso

Postdoctoral Researcher University Hospital Bonn (UKB) / German Center for Neurodegenerative Diseases (DZNE) karla.valdivieso@dzne.de

My research focuses on spatial omics approaches, cellular senescence, and the mechanisms of aging, with particular emphasis on tissue regeneration and age-associated dysfunction. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher with Prof. Özgün Gökçe, investigating brain aging and neurodegeneration using spatially resolved molecular and cellular approaches. I previously worked with Prof. Diana Jurk at the Mayo Clinic, studying the role of senescence in aging and age-related disease.

I received my Ph.D. in Life Sciences in 2024 at the University of Natural Resources and Life Sciences (BOKU Vienna, Austria) with a dissertation on the relationship between senescent cells and wound healing (supervisor: Dr. Mikołaj Ogrodnik, Ludwig Boltzmann Research Group on Senescence and Healing of Wounds).

Publications

Selected works
1. Transcription-independent induction of rapid-onset senescence is integral to healing
Valdivieso, K., Rozmaric, T., Victorelli, S., et al. (2026). Nature Cell Biology.
First demonstration that rapid-onset senescence operates independently of de novo transcription during wound repair, redefining the mechanistic basis of beneficial senescence.
2. Tomatidine is a senotherapeutic compound that improves cognitive function and reduces cellular senescence in aged mice
Costa, D.G., ..., Valdivieso, K., et al. (2026). EMBO Molecular Medicine. 18, 1530–1550.
Identifies tomatidine as a novel senolytic with translational potential for cognitive aging.
3. The Sixth Annual Symposium of the Midwest Aging Consortium
Sturmlechner, I., Ashiqueali, S.A., ..., Valdivieso, K., et al. (2025). The Journals of Gerontology: Series A. 81(3), glaf280.
Consortium proceedings reflecting cross-institutional aging research network activity.
4. Platform for drug testing and studying rapid-onset signaling and induction of cellular phenotypes ex vivo
Valdivieso, K., Dworak, H., Schädl, B., et al. (2025). Journal of Investigative Dermatology. 145, 3237–3240.
Describes a reproducible ex vivo skin platform enabling rapid screening of senescence-inducing compounds; directly applicable to drug discovery pipelines in dermatology and aging research.
5. The p-rpS6-zone delineates wounding responses and the healing process
Ring, N.A.R., Dworak, H., ..., Valdivieso, K., et al. (2023). Developmental Cell. 58, 981–992.
Discovery of a spatially distinct rpS6-phosphorylation zone governing wound response patterning.
6. The role of senescence in cellular plasticity: Lessons from regeneration and development and implications for age-related diseases
Ring, N.A.R., Valdivieso, K., Grillari, J., Redl, H., Ogrodnik, M. (2022). Developmental Cell. 57, 1083–1101.
Insights on senescence and plasticity in regeneration and development. BOKU Best Paper Award 2023.
7. Promises and challenges of senolytics in skin regeneration, pathology and ageing
Pils, V., Ring, N.A.R., Valdivieso, K., et al. (2021). Mechanisms of Ageing and Development. 200, 111588.
Critical appraisal of senolytic strategies in the skin; widely cited in the senescence and wound healing literature.
